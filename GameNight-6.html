First Half Analysis

Manchester United started the match with tremendous intensity, pressing high and forcing Liverpool into uncomfortable positions. The home side's energy and organization paid dividends early on.

The opening goal came in the 12th minute when Marcus Rashford demonstrated clinical finishing. Casemiro's incisive through ball split the Liverpool defense, and Rashford made no mistake, rounding goalkeeper Alisson Becker before slotting home with characteristic composure.

United continued to dominate possession and territory throughout the first half. Their second goal arrived just before the break when Bruno Fernandes produced a moment of magic, unleashing a spectacular strike from 25 yards that flew into the top corner, giving Alisson no chance.

At halftime, Manchester United held a commanding 2-0 lead, and Old Trafford was electric with anticipation of a famous victory over their historic rivals.

Second Half Comeback

The second half saw a completely transformed Liverpool side. Manager Jürgen Klopp's halftime adjustments proved decisive as the Reds emerged with renewed purpose and intensity.

Liverpool's comeback began just seven minutes after the restart. Trent Alexander-Arnold delivered a perfect cross from the right flank, and Cody Gakpo rose highest to power a header past André Onana. The goal immediately shifted momentum and belief.

The Reds continued to press forward relentlessly. Darwin Núñez equalized in the 67th minute with a display of strength and skill, holding off Harry Maguire's challenge before firing a low shot into the bottom corner. Old Trafford fell silent as Liverpool drew level.

The decisive moment arrived in the 83rd minute. Mohamed Salah collected the ball on the right side of the penalty area, cut inside onto his favored left foot, and curled a magnificent shot beyond Onana's reach into the far corner. The Egyptian's celebration was one of pure elation as Liverpool completed an extraordinary turnaround.

Post-Match Reactions

Liverpool manager Jürgen Klopp praised his team's character: "At halftime, we knew we needed to be much better. The response in the second half was incredible. This is what this team is about – we never give up, and today we showed that fighting spirit."

Manchester United boss Erik ten Hag expressed disappointment: "We were excellent in the first half and should have gone in with an even bigger lead. But we switched off in key moments after halftime, and Liverpool punished us. We need to learn how to close out games against top opposition."

Mohamed Salah, speaking after scoring the winner, said: "This is why we play football – for moments like this. Old Trafford is a tough place to come, but we believed we could turn it around. The team showed incredible spirit, and I'm proud to contribute the winning goal."
